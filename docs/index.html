Northeastern University

Yanzhi Wang is a Professor in the College of Engineering at Northeastern University, holding a courtesy appointment in the Khoury College of Computer Sciences. He previously served as an Assistant Professor at Syracuse University before joining Northeastern in Fall 2018.

He earned his Ph.D. in Computer Engineering from the University of Southern California in 2014, where he received the Ming Hsieh Scholar Award. He obtained a B.S. in Electronic Engineering from Tsinghua University in 2009 with distinction.

Research Interests

  • Real-time and energy-efficient deep learning and artificial intelligence systems
  • Large language models and large-scale generative AI systems
  • Model compression and mobile acceleration of deep neural networks (DNNs)
  • Deep learning acceleration for autodriving
  • Neuromorphic computing and non-von Neumann computing paradigms
  • Cyber-security in deep learning systems
