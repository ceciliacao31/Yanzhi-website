Yanzhi Wang

Associate Professor, Northeastern University

Yanzhi Wang is an Associate Professor in the College of Engineering at Northeastern University, holding a courtesy appointment in the Khoury College of Computer Sciences. He previously served as an Assistant Professor at Syracuse University before joining Northeastern in Fall 2018.

He earned his Ph.D. in Computer Engineering from the University of Southern California in 2014, where he received the Ming Hsieh Scholar Award. He obtained a B.S. in Electronic Engineering from Tsinghua University in 2009 with distinction.

Research Interests

Real-time and energy-efficient deep learning and artificial intelligence systems.

  • Deep Learning Acceleration: Model compression (pruning, quantization) and compilation for real-time execution on mobile/edge devices.
  • Efficient LLMs: Large Language Models and Generative AI systems efficiency.
  • Hardware-Software Co-design: Automated co-design for FPGAs and ASICs.
  • Neuromorphic Computing: Non-von Neumann computing paradigms and emerging devices.
  • Secure AI: Adversarial robustness and privacy in deep learning systems.
